What is YLAR

Built by the ones who lived it

Most college counseling programs help students polish their essays. YLAR helps them find their story. We're a free, student-run program built specifically for first-generation, low-income students — by mentors who navigated the same process, felt the same fears, and made it through anyway.

We know what it's like to not understand FAFSA, to feel behind peers who had private consultants, to write essays without anyone in your corner. That lived experience is exactly what makes YLAR different.

Frequently Asked Questions

Yes — completely and always. YLAR was built on the belief that quality college application support shouldn't be reserved for those who can pay for it. There is no cost, no hidden fee, and no catch.
YLAR is designed for first-generation and/or low-income (FGLI) high school students who are preparing to apply to college. If you're a rising senior applying this cycle, you're our primary focus — though we welcome students in earlier grades who want to start preparing.
Most programs offer editing. YLAR offers strategy. Our mentors are current college students who were FGLI applicants themselves — they understand the emotional, financial, and logistical weight of this process in a way traditional counselors can't. We focus on narrative, not just polish.
After you apply, we review your application and match you with a mentor based on your goals, areas of need, and background. We aim to pair students with mentors who share similar experiences or academic interests whenever possible.
It depends on where you are in the process. During peak season (August–January), mentees and mentors typically connect weekly or as needed around deadlines. The commitment is flexible and built around your schedule.
Absolutely not. YLAR supports students applying to all types of schools — state universities, HBCUs, liberal arts colleges, community colleges, and yes, highly selective schools too. What matters is that you're a FGLI student who wants support, not where you're applying.
If you're a current college student who identifies as first-generation and/or low-income, we want to hear from you. Hit the "Become a Mentor" button and fill out our short application. We look for people who are committed, responsive, and genuinely want to give back.
Applications for both mentors and mentees are open now. Mentor onboarding typically happens in the summer, with active mentorship beginning in August ahead of Early Decision and QuestBridge deadlines in the fall.
