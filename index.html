What is YLAR

Built by the ones who lived it

Most college counseling programs help students polish their essays. YLAR helps them find their story. We're a free, student-run program built specifically for first-generation and low-income (FGLI) students.

We know what it's like to not understand FAFSA, to feel behind peers who had private consultants, to write essays without anyone in your corner. That lived experience is exactly why we do this.

01

Making information that's usually gatekept available to everyone — no cost, no barrier, no catch.

Helping students tell their stories with confidence — because clarity and authenticity win, not prestige.

Yes — completely and always. YLAR was built on the belief that quality college application support shouldn't be reserved for those who can pay for it. There is no cost, no upsell, and no strings attached.
YLAR is designed for first-generation and/or low-income (FGLI) high school students who are preparing to apply to college. If you're a rising senior applying this cycle, you're welcome to apply.
Most programs offer editing. YLAR offers strategy. Our mentors are current college students who were FGLI applicants themselves — they understand the emotional, financial, and identity dynamics that shape your application.
After you apply, we review your application and match you with a mentor based on your goals, areas of need, and background. We aim to pair students with mentors who share similar experiences.
It depends on where you are in the process. During peak season (August–January), mentees and mentors typically connect weekly or as needed around deadlines. The time commitment is flexible.
